🔷 Key Frameworks

The core mental models and methodologies taught in this course:

🏗️ Design Thinking (Stanford d.school 5-Stage Model)

Human-centered innovation methodology: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test. An iterative loop, not linear. Popularized by IDEO and David Kelley. Widely taught in top executive programs worldwide.

💎 Double Diamond Framework (Design Council UK)

Two phases of divergent/convergent thinking: Discover → Define (understand the problem) then Develop → Deliver (create the solution). Effective innovation requires both expansive exploration and disciplined focus.

🌊 Blue Ocean Strategy (Kim & Mauborgne, INSEAD)

Create uncontested market space rather than competing in "red oceans." Four Actions Framework (ERRC): Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create. Key tools: Strategy Canvas, Three Tiers of Noncustomers. Named among top 4 thinkers in 100 years of Harvard Business Review.

🏢 IBM Enterprise Design Thinking

Scaled innovation for large organizations. Three principles: Focus on user outcomes, Restless reinvention, Diverse empowered teams. Practices: Hills (outcome-based goals), Playbacks (alignment checkpoints), Sponsor Users (real customers on the team).

🧠 Growth Mindset (Carol Dweck)

Fixed vs. Growth mindset. Organizations with growth mindset cultures encourage risk-taking, view failures as learning opportunities, and foster continuous improvement. Applied by Satya Nadella at Microsoft: from "know-it-all" to "learn-it-all."

🛡️ Psychological Safety (Amy Edmondson, Harvard)

The #1 factor in high-performing teams (Google's Project Aristotle). Essential for creative transformation — enables candid debate, risk-taking, and learning from failure without fear.

🔟 Ten Types of Innovation (Doblin/Deloitte)

Innovation beyond products: Configuration (Profit Model, Network, Structure, Process) + Offering (Product Performance, Product System) + Experience (Service, Channel, Brand, Customer Engagement). Companies using 5+ types outperform significantly.

🏢 Case Studies

Microsoft (2014–present) — Cultural Transformation & Growth Mindset
Nadella shifted culture from "know-it-all" to "learn-it-all." Market cap: $300B → $3T+. Lesson: culture change starts with leadership modeling new behaviors.
LEGO (2004 turnaround) — Creative Turnaround
Near bankruptcy from over-diversification. Applied disciplined creativity: cut 30% of products, engaged fans as co-creators. Revenue: $1B → $9B.
Pixar — Sustained Creative Culture
"Braintrust" candid feedback mechanism, postmortems on every project, protecting new ideas. "Quality is the best business plan."
DBS Bank (2014–present) — Design Thinking at Scale
Transformed from traditional Singapore bank to "World's Best Digital Bank." Redesigned 200+ customer journeys using design thinking methodology.
Nintendo Wii/Switch — Blue Ocean Strategy in Action
Classic ERRC application: eliminated cutting-edge graphics, reduced complexity, raised accessibility, created motion-controlled gaming. Outsold Sony + Microsoft combined.
