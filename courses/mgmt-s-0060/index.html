📋 Official Description: Exploring innovations; the processes used to explore innovations along the technology, market and strategy dimensions; executing innovations; the structures and incentives organizations; exploiting innovations; innovation platforms; renewing innovations.

🔷 Key Frameworks

Core mental models and methodologies for this course:

Effectuation (Saras Sarasvathy)

How expert entrepreneurs ACTUALLY think — not predictive, but creative. 5 principles: Bird-in-Hand (start with what you have), Affordable Loss (risk only what you can lose), Crazy Quilt (build partnerships), Lemonade (leverage surprises), Pilot-in-the-Plane (co-create the future).

Lean Startup (Eric Ries)

Build-Measure-Learn feedback loop. Minimum Viable Product (MVP) — the smallest thing you can build to test a hypothesis. Validated learning over vanity metrics. Pivot or persevere. Applied beyond startups to corporate innovation.

Disciplined Entrepreneurship (Bill Aulet, MIT)

24-step systematic framework from idea to scale. Key steps: Beachhead Market selection, Total Addressable Market calculation, End User Persona, Quantified Value Proposition. Brings rigor to the "fuzzy front end" of innovation.

Explore-Execute-Exploit-Renew Cycle

The course's organizing framework. Explore (design thinking, Blue Ocean, opportunity recognition), Execute (Stage-Gate, ambidextrous organization), Exploit (platforms, network effects, scaling), Renew (dynamic capabilities, creative destruction, corporate entrepreneurship).

Crossing the Chasm (Geoffrey Moore)

Technology Adoption Lifecycle: Innovators → Early Adopters → Early Majority → Late Majority → Laggards. The CHASM is between Early Adopters and Early Majority — where most ventures die. Crossing requires a beachhead strategy.

🏢 Case Studies

Airbnb — Effectual Entrepreneurship
Airbed shortage during a design conference → global platform. Started with what they had (Bird-in-Hand), built partnerships (Crazy Quilt). Classic effectuation in action.
Dropbox — MVP Validation
Explainer video as MVP. 75,000 beta signups overnight — before a single line of production code. Validated demand at near-zero cost.
Tesla — Innovation Platform Strategy
Car as platform, not product. Continuous OTA updates. Battery ecosystem as competitive moat. Vertical integration defying industry orthodoxy.
