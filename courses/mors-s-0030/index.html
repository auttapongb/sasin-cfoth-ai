🔷 Key Frameworks

Core mental models and methodologies for this course:

Transformational Leadership (Bass, 1985)

Four I's: Idealized Influence (be a role model), Inspirational Motivation (create compelling vision), Intellectual Stimulation (encourage innovation), Individualized Consideration (coach each person). Research shows transformational leaders consistently outperform transactional ones.

Situational Leadership II (Hersey & Blanchard)

No single "best" leadership style. Match your approach to the follower's development level: S1 Directing → S2 Coaching → S3 Supporting → S4 Delegating. The same person needs different leadership for different tasks.

Servant Leadership (Greenleaf, 1970)

The leader serves first. 10 principles: Listening, Empathy, Healing, Awareness, Persuasion, Conceptualization, Foresight, Stewardship, Commitment to Growth, Building Community. Widely adopted in values-driven organizations.

Emotional Intelligence (Goleman, 1995)

Four domains: Self-Awareness, Self-Management, Social Awareness, Relationship Management. Research shows EQ is 2× more important than IQ for senior leadership success. Emotions are contagious — the leader's emotional state sets team tone.

Kotter's 8-Step Change Model

1. Create Urgency 2. Build Guiding Coalition 3. Form Strategic Vision 4. Enlist Volunteer Army 5. Enable Action (Remove Barriers) 6. Generate Short-Term Wins 7. Sustain Acceleration 8. Institute Change. 70% of transformations fail by skipping Step 1.

Adaptive Leadership (Heifetz & Linsky, Harvard)

Distinguish technical problems (can be solved by expertise) from adaptive challenges (require changes in values/beliefs/behaviors). Key: "Get on the balcony" to see patterns, regulate distress, give the work back to people.

Gary Yukl (9th ed, 2019) — The most comprehensive academic text on leadership. Title matches this course.
Leadership: Theory and Practice
Peter Northouse (9th ed, 2021) — The other dominant graduate text. Case-based with self-assessments.
Dare to Lead
Brené Brown (2018) — Vulnerability as courage. BRAVING inventory: Boundaries, Reliability, Accountability, Vault, Integrity, Nonjudgment, Generosity.
The Culture Map
Erin Meyer (2014) — Essential for Asian EMBA context. How culture shapes leadership expectations across countries.
Leaders Eat Last
Simon Sinek (2014) — Circle of Safety. Neurobiology of trust: oxytocin, cortisol, and why great leaders protect their teams.
Leading Change
John P. Kotter (1996) — The classic text on organizational transformation. Still the standard 30 years later.

🏢 Case Studies

DBS Bank — Leadership Transformation
CEO Piyush Gupta transformed Singaporean bank culture from bureaucratic to startup-like. "Making banking joyful."
New Zealand PM Jacinda Ardern
Modern example of compassionate leadership during crisis. Empathy + decisiveness. "They are us" after Christchurch.
