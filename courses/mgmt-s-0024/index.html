📋 Official Description: Explores the critical role of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) factors in shaping business strategies in the 21st century. As sustainability, ethical leadership, and corporate responsibility become key drivers of long-term success, businesses must navigate evolving ESG frameworks, regulatory requirements, and stakeholder expectations.

🔷 Key Frameworks

Core mental models and methodologies for this course:

GRI, SASB, TCFD & ISSB Standards

Global reporting frameworks: GRI (comprehensive sustainability), SASB (industry-specific metrics), TCFD (climate risk disclosure), ISSB (IFRS S1/S2 global baseline). Thailand requires SEC Form 56-1 One Report aligned with these.

UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

17 interconnected goals adopted by 193 countries in 2015. The universal framework for measuring corporate impact on poverty, climate, equality, and governance. Most Fortune 500 companies now report against SDGs.

Double Materiality

Two lenses: Financial materiality (ESG risks/opportunities affecting company value) AND Impact materiality (company's effects on environment and society). Required under EU CSRD. A paradigm shift from single materiality.

Stakeholder Capitalism

Shift from shareholder primacy (Friedman doctrine) to multi-stakeholder value creation. Key stakeholders: employees, communities, environment, suppliers, customers — alongside shareholders. Business Roundtable 2019 declaration.

Circular Economy & Net Zero

Design out waste, keep products/materials in use, regenerate natural systems. Carbon footprint Scopes: Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (energy), Scope 3 (supply chain). Net Zero = balance emissions produced and removed.

ESG Integration in Finance

Green bonds, sustainability-linked loans, ESG ratings (MSCI, Sustainalytics). Thai banking leadership: Bank of Ayudhya, Kasikornbank, SCB pioneering sustainable finance in ASEAN.

🏢 Case Studies

PTTGC — Circular Economy & Net-Zero
Thai petrochemical leader transitioning to circular economy. Net-zero 2050 commitment. A model for ASEAN industrial transformation.
SCG/SCGP — Circular Packaging Innovation
Siam Cement Group's packaging arm pioneering recycled/compostable materials. Sustainability as competitive advantage.
TRUE Corporation — S&P Global Gold Class
Thai telecom achieving top ESG ratings through comprehensive sustainability programs embedded in operations.
Ørsted — From Black to Green Energy
Danish company transformed from fossil fuel utility to world's largest offshore wind developer. Formerly DONG Energy.
Patagonia — Earth as Shareholder
Founder Yvon Chouinard gave away the company to fight climate crisis. 'Earth is now our only shareholder.' Radical stakeholder capitalism.
