Section A: Introduction & Overview

Chapter 1 — What Is Strategy and Why Is It Important?

Strategy = management's game plan to stake out a market position, attract customers, conduct operations, compete, and achieve performance targets.

5-Phase Strategy Process

PhaseDescription
1. Strategic VisionLong-term direction — where are we going?
2. Set ObjectivesFinancial + strategic goals (Balanced Scorecard)
3. Craft StrategyRespond to environment, competitive moves, build capabilities
4. Implement & ExecuteOperations-oriented — make things happen
5. Evaluate & AdjustCorrective actions based on results

Strategy is collaborative: EDS involved 2,500 employees in strategy review. J.M. Smucker used 140 employees over 6 months.

Levels: Single-business = 3 levels (business → functional → operating). Diversified = 4 levels (+ corporate at top).

StrategyHow It WorksBest When
1. Overall Low-Cost Meaningfully lower costs than rivals — control cost drivers or reengineer value chain Products identical/weakly differentiated, price-sensitive buyers, low switching costs
2. Broad Differentiation Incorporate attributes buyers value and pay premium for Diverse buyer preferences, few rivals using similar approach, rapid innovation
3. Best-Cost Provider Match rivals on quality + beat them on cost = "more value for money" Buyer diversity + price sensitivity coexist
4. Focused Low-Cost Serve niche buyers at lower cost than broad competitors Profitable niche, costly for broad competitors to serve
5. Focused Differentiation Specialized offering for niche buyers Niche matches focuser's strengths, few rivals in same segment

Chapter 6 — Strengthening Competitive Position

Strategic moves beyond generic strategies: timing (first-mover advantages), offensive vs defensive, M&A, strategic alliances.

Chapter 7 — International Markets

Entry modes: exporting → licensing → franchising → joint ventures → wholly-owned subsidiaries. Multi-country vs global strategies.

Chapter 8 — Corporate Diversification

Three Tests Every Diversification Must Pass
1. Attractiveness TestIs the industry structurally attractive?
2. Cost-of-Entry TestWill future profits cover the entry price?
3. Better-Off TestWill the combined entity be stronger? (1+1=3)
Related DiversificationUnrelated Diversification
Strategic fits across value chainsAny profitable business on good terms
Transfer skills, share activitiesSpread risk across industries
Leverage brand nameFinancial gain from undervalued assets
Build competitive advantageRisk: too many businesses = complexity

6-Step Evaluation: ① Evaluate industry attractiveness → ② Assess competitive strength → ③ Check cross-business fits → ④ Check resource fit → ⑤ Rank prospects → ⑥ Craft new moves

Chapter 9 — Ethics, CSR & Sustainability

Triple bottom line (People, Planet, Profit). Moral case + business case for ethical behavior. CSR as strategic imperative.
