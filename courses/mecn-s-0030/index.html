📋 Official Description: Tools of economic analysis applicable to managerial decisions; market structures; equilibrium; efficiency; externalities; demand; costs; marginal revenue; scale economies; economic growth, monetary and fiscal policy and international trade; network effects; moral hazard and adverse selection; price discrimination.

🔷 Key Frameworks

Core mental models and methodologies for this course:

Supply & Demand + Elasticity

Foundation of all market analysis. Price elasticity of demand: %ΔQ / %ΔP. Elastic (>1) vs inelastic (<1). Determinants: substitutes, necessity vs luxury, time horizon, budget share. Consumer surplus, producer surplus, deadweight loss.

Market Structures Spectrum

Perfect Competition (many firms, identical products, price takers) → Monopolistic Competition (differentiation) → Oligopoly (few firms, strategic interdependence, game theory) → Monopoly (one firm, barriers to entry). Concentration ratios and HHI measure structure.

Marginal Analysis

The economic way of thinking: MR = MC optimization. Marginal benefit = marginal cost at the optimum. Sunk costs should be ignored. Break-even analysis. Make-or-buy decisions. Opportunity cost — the value of the next best alternative foregone.

Externalities & Market Failure

Positive/negative externalities: costs/benefits that affect third parties not reflected in market prices. Solutions: Pigouvian taxes, Coase theorem (private bargaining with clear property rights), regulation, cap-and-trade. Tragedy of the commons and public goods.

Game Theory & Strategic Behavior

Nash equilibrium: each player's strategy is optimal given others' strategies. Prisoner's Dilemma: individually rational behavior leads to collectively worse outcomes. Applications: price wars, OPEC coordination, R&D races. Sequential games and credible commitments.

Macroeconomic Policy & Growth

Monetary policy (central bank: interest rates, money supply, inflation targeting). Fiscal policy (government spending, taxation, public debt). GDP components: C + I + G + (X − M). Business cycles, unemployment types, Phillips curve trade-off.
