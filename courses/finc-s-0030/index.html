🔷 Key Frameworks

Core mental models and methodologies for this course:

Time Value of Money (TVM)

The foundation of all finance. Present Value (PV), Future Value (FV), annuities, perpetuities. Discounting future cash flows to the present. The concept: a dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow. NPV (Net Present Value) is the cornerstone capital budgeting rule.

Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)

Expected Return = Risk-Free Rate + β × (Market Return − Risk-Free Rate). Security Market Line (SML): plots expected return against systematic risk (beta). Used to calculate cost of equity and required rate of return. Distinguishes systematic (market) risk from unsystematic (diversifiable) risk.

Bond Valuation & Interest Rates

Bond price = PV of future coupon payments + PV of face value. Inverse relationship: when interest rates rise, bond prices fall. Duration measures price sensitivity to rate changes. Yield to Maturity (YTM): the discount rate that makes PV of cash flows = market price.

Stock Valuation Methods

1. Dividend Discount Model (Gordon Growth): P = D₁/(r−g). 2. Discounted Free Cash Flow (FCFF/FCFE). 3. Relative Valuation: P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA multiples. No single method is "correct" — triangulate using multiple approaches.

Capital Budgeting Decision Rules

NPV (accept if > 0) — theoretically correct. IRR (accept if > cost of capital) — intuitive but flawed for non-conventional cash flows. Payback Period — simple but ignores time value of money. Profitability Index — useful when capital is constrained. Real options analysis for strategic flexibility.
