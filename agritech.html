Dr. Cavite's research centers on marketing and management research in agribusiness — consumers' purchase intentions, farmers' technology adoption, organic products, and product traceability. With 10+ years of combined experience spanning Philippine government agriculture (Department of Agriculture, PhilRice) to Thai academia (KMITL, Sasin), his work connects directly to UN SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production. He teaches marketing research in the Flexible MBA and quantitative research in the DBA program.

ESG × Agriculture in ASEAN

🌍
Environmental (E)
▾
  • Carbon: Agriculture = ~19% of SEA emissions (deforestation, rice methane, livestock)
  • Water: Rice uses 40% of freshwater; AWD technology reduces by 30%
  • Soil: Chemical overuse degrading 40% of agricultural land
  • Biodiversity: Deforestation for palm oil/rubber; RSPO certification growing
  • Climate adaptation: Mangrove restoration, drought-resistant seeds, precision irrigation
👥
Social (S)
▾
  • Livelihoods: ~100 million smallholder farmers in ASEAN
  • Food security: Rice self-sufficiency concerns as urbanization expands
  • Labor rights: Aging farm workforce (avg. age 50+ in Thailand)
  • Gender: Women ~50% of ag labor but limited land ownership
  • Community enterprise: Prof. Cavite's research focus — cooperative models for organic transition
⚖️
Governance (G)
▾
  • Traceability: EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) requires geolocation proof
  • Certifications: GlobalG.A.P., Organic, Fair Trade, MSC — market access requirements
  • Policy: Thailand's BCG Model, Indonesia's Stratnas AI, Vietnam's green agriculture strategy
  • ESG reporting: Becoming mandatory for export markets; SMEs increasingly adopting
  • Key tension: Global ESG requirements vs. smallholder capacity to comply

SWOT Analysis: AgriTech in Southeast Asia

💪
Strengths
▾
  • Agricultural heritage: Deep traditional knowledge; rice = cultural backbone
  • Biodiversity: Diverse agro-ecological zones; multiple crop systems
  • Youth demographics: Large tech-savvy population in urban areas
  • Export dominance: World's top exporter of rice, palm oil, rubber, seafood
  • Government support: National agritech strategies (Thailand 4.0, Indonesia Stratnas AI)
  • Digital infrastructure: 90%+ mobile penetration; expanding 4G/5G
  • Low labor costs: Competitive advantage for labor-intensive crops
⚠️
Weaknesses
▾
  • Smallholder fragmentation: Average farm <2 ha; difficult to scale
  • Aging farmers: Average age 50+ in Thailand; Japan-like trajectory
  • Low digital literacy: Rural farmers lack tech skills for precision agriculture
  • Post-harvest losses: 30–40% due to poor storage and logistics
  • Limited R&D: Ag R&D <1% of agricultural GDP in most SEA countries
  • Financial exclusion: Limited credit for smallholders; high-interest informal lending
  • Infrastructure gaps: Rural roads, cold storage, irrigation inconsistent
🚀
Opportunities
▾
  • $90B GDP unlock: Digital agritech could add $90B annually by 2033
  • AI revolution: Could contribute $1T to regional economy by 2030
  • ESG premium: Traceable, sustainable products command 10–30% price premiums
  • Carbon markets: Credits from regenerative agriculture, avoided deforestation
  • ASEAN integration: RCEP, EVFTA open export markets requiring higher standards
  • Fintech-agritech convergence: Mobile payments, micro-insurance, digital lending
  • Climate adaptation tech: Drought-resistant seeds, water-saving irrigation, early warning
  • Alternative proteins: Growing middle-class demand for sustainable protein
🔴
Threats
▾
  • Climate change: -7% to -9% cereal yields by 2050; Mekong saltwater intrusion
  • Extreme weather: Increasing typhoons, floods — $21B losses (2008–2018)
  • Water scarcity: Competition from industry/urban use; groundwater depletion
  • Land conversion: Agricultural land lost to urbanization at accelerating rate
  • Global competition: Brazil, India, Africa competing on commodity crops
  • Trade barriers: EUDR, carbon border taxes — compliance costs for smallholders
  • Funding winter: Agritech VC down ~70%; eFishery scandal damaged trust
  • Brain drain: Rural youth migrating to cities; farm labor shortage

AgriTech Case Studies

Discussion Questions to Prepare

Built around Prof. Cavite's published research — connecting his work to broader themes.

1. Technology Adoption Gap
▾
Prof. Cavite's research shows organic rice adoption is far below policy targets despite incentives. What behavioral, economic, and information barriers explain this? How would you apply this framework to agritech adoption in general?
2. Consumer-Driven Sustainability
▾
His 2025 paper asks whether environmental concerns drive millennials' organic purchases. How can agritech companies leverage consumer ESG demand to create pull (not just push) for sustainable farming?
3. Smallholder vs. Corporate
▾
Can agritech serve both 33M Indonesian smallholders AND large agribusinesses — or do solutions need to be fundamentally different?
4. ESG as Trade Barrier or Opportunity?
▾
EUDR requires deforestation-free supply chains — will this exclude smallholders or incentivize them to adopt traceability tech?
5. Thailand's AgriTech Priority
▾
As a top rice exporter with aging farmers, what agritech solutions should Thailand prioritize? How does Prof. Cavite's research on Thai organic rice inform this?
6. SWOT Application
▾
Pick one SEA country — conduct a rapid SWOT of its agritech potential. What's the single most leverageable strength and most critical threat?
7. The $90B Question
▾
Digital agritech could unlock $90B, but venture funding has collapsed 70% since 2022. What needs to change to unlock this capital?
8. Carbon Markets for Smallholders
▾
Can ASEAN smallholders realistically access carbon credit markets? What infrastructure is needed?
